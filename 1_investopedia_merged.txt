Preliminary Investigation and Its Activities

1. Problem Identification and Definition

Many individuals, especially those new to investing, struggle to make informed decisions about
where and how to invest their savings, such as 10 lakhs. The lack of personalized guidance,
real-time market insights, and curated knowledge often leads to suboptimal investment choices,
resulting in lower returns or higher risks.

2. Problem Description

Investors face challenges like information overload, difficulty understanding complex financial
products, and lack of timely updates on market trends. Existing platforms may provide raw
data but often fail to offer tailored advice or integrate multiple investment options in a user-
friendly manner. This gap makes it hard for users to confidently allocate their funds across
various instruments like stocks, mutual funds, fixed deposits, or bonds.

3. Fact Finding Techniques

3.1. Observation
Observing potential users interacting with current investment platforms reveals confusion due
to complex interfaces and overwhelming data. Many users spend excessive time researching
without clear direction, indicating a need for simplified, guided investment advice.
3.2. Interviews
Conducting interviews with novice investors, financial advisors, and platform users uncovers
common pain points such as lack of trust in automated recommendations, difficulty

understanding jargon, and desire for personalized investment plans aligned with individual risk
profiles and goals.
3.3. Questionnaires/Surveys
Surveys distributed among college students, young professionals, and middle-aged investors
highlight that a significant percentage feel under-informed about investment options and
express interest in a platform that offers curated knowledge, real-time insights, and easy-to-
understand guidance.

3.4. Document Review
Reviewing financial reports, market analysis, and existing investment apps' documentation
shows that while data availability is high, integration and personalization remain limited.
Regulatory guidelines also emphasize the importance of investor education and transparency,
which current systems partially address.

4. Drawbacks of Existing Systems

    À Lack of personalized investment advice tailored to individual goals and risk tolerance.
    À Overwhelming and complex user interfaces that intimidate beginners.
    À Insufficient real-time insights and alerts to help users react promptly to market changes.
    À Limited integration of diverse investment options in a single platform.
    À Minimal educational content to help users understand investment fundamentals.

5. Scope for the Proposed System

The proposed Smart Investment Guide Platform aims to:
    À Provide personalized investment recommendations based on user profiles and goals.

6. Feasibility Study

Technical Feasibility:
The platform can be developed using existing technologies such as data analytics, machine
learning for personalized recommendations, and APIs for real-time market data. Cloud
infrastructure can support scalability and security.

    Economic Feasibility:
Initial development costs are moderate, with potential revenue streams from premium features,
advisory services, or partnerships with financial institutions. The growing interest in personal
finance apps suggests a viable market.

    Operational Feasibility:
Users are increasingly comfortable with digital financial tools. The platform's educational
focus and ease of use will encourage adoption. Continuous updates and customer support will
ensure sustained engagement.

    Legal Feasibility:
Compliance with financial regulations and data privacy laws will be ensured through proper
legal consultation and adherence to guidelines.

                Requirement Specification

1. Data Requirement of the System

The system requires various types of data to function effectively and provide meaningful
investment guidance:

À User Profile Data: Personal details such as age, income, investment goals, risk tolerance,
   and investment horizon.

À Market Data: Real-time and historical data on stocks, mutual funds, bonds, fixed deposits,
   and other investment instruments. This includes prices, returns, volatility, and market news.

À Financial Product Information: Details about different investment options including
   interest rates, maturity periods, fund performance, fees, and terms & conditions.

À Educational Content: Curated articles, tutorials, FAQs, and glossaries to help users
   understand investment concepts.

À Transaction Data: Records of user investments, portfolio allocations, and transaction
   history for tracking and analysis.

À Regulatory and Compliance Data: Information related to financial regulations to ensure
   recommendations comply with legal standards.

2. Identify End Users of the System

The platform is designed to serve a diverse group of users, including:

À Novice Investors: Individuals with little or no prior investment experience seeking
   guidance to start investing their savings wisely.

À Experienced Investors: Users who want to optimize their portfolios with real-time
   insights and personalized recommendations.

À Financial Advisors: Professionals who may use the platform to assist clients with curated
   knowledge and data-driven advice.

À Students and Learners: Individuals interested in improving their financial literacy and
   understanding investment fundamentals.

À Retirees and Senior Citizens: Users looking for safe and stable investment options
   aligned with their retirement goals.

À Real-Time Alerts: Notifications about market movements, investment opportunities, or

   risks.
À Educational Insights: Easy-to-understand articles, tips, and tutorials relevant to the user's

   investment journey.
À Performance Reports: Periodic summaries showing portfolio growth, returns, and risk

   metrics.
À Comparative Analysis: Side-by-side comparison of different investment options to aid

   decision-making.

5.1. Functional Requirements
À User registration and profile management.
À Risk assessment questionnaire to determine user risk tolerance.
À Integration with real-time market data providers.
À Algorithm to generate personalized investment recommendations.
À Dashboard to display portfolio status, alerts, and educational content.

À Secure transaction and investment tracking module.
À Feedback mechanism for users to rate recommendations and content.

5.2. Non-Functional Requirements
À Usability: Intuitive and user-friendly interface accessible on web and mobile devices.
À Performance: Fast response times for data retrieval and recommendation generation.
À Scalability: Ability to handle growing numbers of users and data volume without

   degradation.
À Security: Robust data protection, encryption, and compliance with privacy laws to

   safeguard user information.
À Reliability: High availability with minimal downtime and accurate data feeds.
À Maintainability: Modular design to allow easy updates and addition of new features.

5.3. Processing Requirements
À Real-time processing of market data to update recommendations and alerts promptly. À

   Batch processing for generating periodic performance reports and educational content
   updates.
À Data validation and error handling to ensure input accuracy and system stability.
À Machine learning or rule-based algorithms to analyze user data and market trends for
   personalized advice.
